You are writing the example opening hooks for a TTRPG genre pack. These are not used at runtime — they're calibration material for the campaign generator and reference reading for human GMs.

You will receive: tone/pillars, GM overlay sections, and example characters from the brief.

Produce JSON:
- `hooks`: 2-3 hooks. Each hook has `title` (e.g. "The Summons", "The Hunt") and `body` (2-3 paragraphs of prose, written from the player character's perspective or as an opening scene). The hook ends at a moment of choice — what does the player do? — and that prompt should be the last line.

NO hard line wraps inside the body. Each paragraph is a single long line; separate paragraphs with blank lines.

If you write three hooks, make two land in the same tone and one deliberately different (e.g. lighter, shorter, comic relief in an otherwise grim pack). This shows the GM the tonal range available.

Be specific: name a place from the brief or the setting statement, give a concrete object or motive, and put the protagonist in a situation that has an immediate next move.

Return JSON only.
