## Review checklist for Symbaroum Dark Fantasy pack (story-mode)

### Tone

- [ ] Does the GM consistently describe taint physically (sensory) — sweat, metal-and-honey, wet rot — rather than as a counter or label?
- [ ] Does the GM treat inquisitors as morally complex rather than cartoon villains?
- [ ] Does the GM resist the urge to resolve tension easily?
- [ ] Do NPCs have distinct voices, or are they blurring together?
- [ ] Does the GM lean on the character's stated advantages and disadvantages rather than improvising new traits?

### Adjudication without dice

- [ ] Are clean successes rare and earned? (Default is success with a cost.)
- [ ] When a disadvantage is in play, does the GM commit to failure or partial success rather than soften?
- [ ] Does the GM use the genre's complications list, or is it falling back to generic "you fail"?

### Truths and reveals

- [ ] When no Director's note is in the AN, does the GM resist hinting at underlying campaign truths?
- [ ] When a Director's note is in the AN, does the GM find an in-fiction moment for the truth rather than dumping it?
- [ ] Are accumulated taint signs eventually paid off with a permanent change?

### Content safety

- [ ] Has the player reviewed content_to_avoid and agreed with it?
- [ ] Any additions specific to this campaign?

### Playtest items (after session 1)

- [ ] Which complications actually fired? Which never did?
- [ ] Are the advantage/disadvantage phrases the player picked specific enough that the GM recognizes them in play?
- [ ] Did taint signs land as accumulating dread or come across as boilerplate?
- [ ] Did any Director's notes land well? Any that the player missed entirely?

### Pack-generator items (if this pack was regenerated, not hand-written)

- [ ] The advantages_disadvantages vocabulary has variety across bodily / knowledge / mystical / social axes.
- [ ] The complications list has ~12 items and at least 3 are specific to Davokar/Symbaroum rather than universal.
- [ ] No leftover references to attributes, abilities, resources, or rolls anywhere in the pack files.
